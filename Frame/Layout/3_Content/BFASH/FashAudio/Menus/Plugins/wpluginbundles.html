Default-Paid

If Default Plug-ins are so Wonderful why use anything other or let alone pay for them?

The thing with Audio Plug-ins is they are tools. Each brand has a version of that tool - some are better than others being R&D and "Modeled". Others simply re-imagine the concept, some are Dedicated to aesthetic Purity, others on Character, some on Color (this is normally default to bells and shelves).

An example would be SSL and VC.

These are the core of Music Fidelity. Yet, each sounds a bit different. Just as if you bought hardware and you compare your hardware with a friends with the same exact hardware. You would get 2 different sounds extremely subtle, but different.
Over time those differences will grow - one will be kept in perfect condition - others will have gone through a much more vigorous life cycle creating all sorts of new hums, slight buzzes, weak knobs, calibrations, etc.

In Audio those tiny little changes end up being MASSIVE differences where the beat up version adds a whole "new flavor". Where 4 became 6, and 18db became 10. These little tweaks drastically change the equipments sound overall, due to the very way it is used and set is radically different to the exact same version. So the question is does it make it better than the other? No, Yes, Sometimes, Maybe, IDK.

Subtle

For instance, when a Plug-in gets modeled, its source is Modeled from Particular Boards/HardWare.
Such as in Waves there is SSL Package with 2 versions(G Version and E Version) one has a brown knob one doesn't.

I want to pause here because this is the best example. What is different between just the E & G Series?

We dont have to look far and wide between corporations, or even plugins to see this "HYPEs" tenticales. Here we have the same Corporation, with the Very Same Plugin, Modeling the Very same Board, with the only aesthetic blantant to notice is one is that modern sleek black background and one is Grey.

Yet, the Bells and Shelves are radically different. The G Series - My perferred is much more Broad which is more musical in my opioion.

The E series is much more narrow and "surgical" and albeit sounds way better with Rock. This means if you use the same exact settings on both they will sound different. On E-Series you will need to broaden the band, of the G you would need to narrow, even if you match them identically they still sound different.

Now ask yourself which one is better? Thats the difference and yes they do sound ever so slightly different. E has more clarity & presence, G has more harmonic & body

However, by default this alone will INFLUENCE what you are makeing.

Subtle, but Audio is a ton of Subtle movements.

Plug-ins comedown to more about MINDSET than they are Digital Tools, although are Digital Tools. This is Important to understand as we get into Technology.

Plug-in Shine

The sound, quality, and capabilities of Emulations/Plug-ins will all depend on the quality of Audio you Input, if you put in low quality audio, Plug-ins - nor Hardware will help you. The Chain of Import of Audio or the Quality of Audio sent to you - is Crucial in the performance of Plug-ins/HardWare.

This is the reason the SSL version of the SSL is not always better at 6x the cost of Waves/NI for it rolls the top and bottom in a curving beautifully way adding Body. The NI Version adds a Mid Warmth normally for tracks lacking Mid-Range, nice when I use alot on Transitions. As the Waves Version is neat as it adds a top and sort of thin/tinny element which shines on ridiculously compressed mp3 of most newbies who tend to destroy.

All helpful but these Corrective aspects is not what these Plugins were designed to do, therefore their ins a natural character by simply loading them.

There is a fair argument that EQ is just EQ, which is not essentially true. If none of these work I will turn to FabFilters Q3, it is crucial to understand once you enter this realm of depth the audio quality simply sucks. Drawing in an EQ is great to have on hand if you are being Creative in soundscaping, or room eliminating corrective eq(avoid 95% of this with Analogue), not for mixing songs.

What makes Analogue so great is the pre-selected best freq/range gives limited options, An audio Source should sound good going through any plugin, what you are looking for is computer useage, and listening how it grabs, releases, and best handles transients. Some plugs will sound better on a source than others, with limited options you can shoot them out. Simple oversampleing will provide radical difference in a-b, if you exhaust options, try something else. IMHO, once you get surgical with it, your mindset is nolonger in the song, your a-b option is eliminated, and you begin mixing with your eyes... huge mistake.

The better the source Input Audio the better ALL Plug-ins work. Same as with the Hardware. You would not use a $10 mic through a Plugin 3x that or a Vocal Chain worth say 500-1k dollars - thats like putting Syrup on a Potato and expecting a Lays Potato Chip. If you are working with High Quality Audio then Plug-ins are just as good as the Hardware as it moves towards the Mixing(Engineer), but if your source audio is Low Quality then Plug-ins/Hardware will help you and normally rejected by the Mixer and sent back to the Recording Stage (Studio) to be redone/recaptured - nothing will help except to work on getting Higher Quality Audio.

Melda

Melda is much like Univeral Audio(UAD) and Waves Audio when it comes to all encompassing workflow and/or bundles. They offer literally everything.

Unlike hardware reliant UAD which is a buy in system workflow and unlike Waves which dominated the marketing side of things, Melda is much more obscured and reserved.

Will focus on what really makes Melda Standout such as no Subscription Update Plan like Waves has
as well as in many cases much more indepth control than others, No hardware dependence as UAD has, with no Subscription to use like Slate has.

Meldas Subscription is a payment program, Subscribe to Own. Like I said innovations you dont see anywhere else.

Featured like Analogue Obsession these are free introductory to the Melda Workflow.
As well with simply one example of of the power of supporting.

The 2 FREE plugins are a demonstration of how Melda stands out innovationally; where functions that have not even been referenced to this point come into play.

1 plugin is not free, but unlike Inphase of Waves(alt for manual technical); Meldas Inphase version in Automatic although may not work in all instances or complex projects, Meldas will however, knockout everyday Drums and reoccuring nuiances.

A fantastic example of again of the same tool as Waves, but entirely different useage and purposes.

FabFilter

Keep in mind there is no 1 tool for any aspect - Audio is art - the proper tool for the job is the best tool for the project.

Which Brings us to FabFilter.
These simple basic but beautiful functionality variety of plugins are simply more of a workflow to the toolbox.

So what is FabFilter good for? FabFilter plugs shine best in simple clean GUI and like all the plugs that came before it are fantasitic utilitys. Q3 always stands out more than the rest due its its gorgeous UI and direct workflow.

Like Waves Basic without the Beauty of UI, or the Waves Ren Bundle which introduce offers the same thing with a UI package and a few more utilitys, FabFilters UI is unlike so many provideing the same utility you expect with slight varitons of grabby and variable sound.

WAV/MP3 Differences

WAV dominates in Showcase/Release such as Recording/Listening - however with captured frequency comes great filesize. This is not really a problem until rendering, transferring, uploading, downloading, and Mixing. Where the problem caused is by the data of BitRates.

Sure one could compress the WAV to make it smaller - but compression has already reduced the BitRate - therefore taken utility out of WAV. Enter MP3 which is way more versatile. MP3 is the standard default for this very reason, which also gives the option to listen, stream, download on the fly on nearly any device. Although the compromise is normally highly compressed which practically rules out editing and mixing.

All in all WAV should only be used for Recording/Personal Collections/Originals/Production/Showcase.
MP3 on the other hand is for quite everything else except Recording, Editing, or Mixing : which is not exactly true but generally accurate.

Where does this leave OGG?
OGG is a container which provides quite higher quality audio and not as crushed as MP3 in NineNeins Development.
You could use OGG for even further compression - not advised for such little benefit as I recommend to just convert to MP3.

To Understand this we must look a the Compressor Grammar.

Compressor Grammar

The compressor is by far the most asked about and misunderstood of all audio equipment - the compressor is also called a Limiter, De-esser, Gate, Expander, Exciter, Multi-Band, and one can even say a Tape Machine.

In short a compressor is "Everything" relabeled, renamed, and tune designed - to do certain aspects of compression without crushing the Audio.
Look at compressors as custom task built swiss army knife. a Compressor is simple Control of the Dynamics.

Such for example a "Limiter" is simply a term of TYPE of Compression, a Limiter is simply a Strong Compression.

Another example we see with the Deesser which is a Combined Eq(Correction)/Compression into 1 making it possible to hone in(EQ) and activate the compressor to compress only certain frequencies.

A Gate/Expander is the Combination of Correction/Compressor to turn off and on the Audio - by Sound level(Volume).
In which if volume does not reach a certain threshold(level) the Audio will not come thru - or if the Audio reachs a certain Threshold(volume) the audio is Compressed back down to set level. Allowing one to control the Sound that may pass through and when audio must be reducrd back down keeping a constant level Recording.
Which helps to block out back ground noise.

Notice the Compressor is the main tool here - each aspect is an Adjective describing the Nouns shape, volume, frequency, etc. Hence here we create Vocal Chains and separate the task of the Compressor, with other task to handle specific designed compressors that can lightly touch and effect the audio, before moving on to the next Compressor/Device in the chain.

Example : say we want to remove the 2.5k fequency - you could use an EQ but that would be a permanent change. Instead, we can use a Gate to control Threshold, depending on the source you may just want to tap that 2.5k down 2 db at a certain threshold automatically - enter band Compression. We will take that very signal and once the audio passes through that "band Compression" we will can then add another "compressor" after for a "blanket compression" that compensates for the 2.5k level drop in a single constant waveform.

If more drastic EQ needs to be done we can grab and add an EQ to this chain between whereever needed for sculpting - if not then using these 3 compressors works better for situations such as live and podcast.

This creates the difference of a Strip(Channel). Normally that flows EQ, Dyn(Dynamics-Compression/Gate), Buss(Compression).
Which grants option of Parallax-Compression. which is outside the scope here.

Notice how all this is adjective, that is exactly what the Compressor is doing.

The World of Graphics

Most documentation for particualr applications is pretty straight forward - all thats really left is learning the language.
As long as you know what you seek to acheive, the limits of your vision, and familiar with the pattern of language - Graphics becomes a breeze.

This section is the absolute basics - of learning the language.
The language here is mostly just file types/extensions.

Therefore think of "Graphics" language as a set of purposes. No different than the language you speak.
Such as "Apartment", "House", and "Storage" for example - this takes in consideration space and objective.
This will create your "Type" of "Extension".

If you are hanging ArtWork on the Wall of your House you want the absolute Best Quality(JPEG) Original(Resolution/Framing) for display. Meanwhile, if for your apartment is to small you can get a print out(Compressed) of the Original to fit anywhere you want to display it(PNG)
Its all just purpose.

The language will determine when to switch between Types and why you should as well - as also when you don't have to.
Lets focus on just the Main types.

The combination of these extensions creates the language - the combination of languages displays each Graphic at its optimal resolution.

In Reverse
Does Concept
1. JPG Is a Full Image normally at highest Resolution. Think of JPG as a MasterPiece.
2. PNG Is a Layered Image normally with transparent Resolution. Think of PNG as Advertisement.
3. WEBP creates a Web Friendly Image Compressed for the Web. Think of WEBP as Cloud Storage.
4. GIF creates an Animation. Think of GIF as an Animated Image.
5. SVG creates a Scalable Graphic. Think of SVG as a Responsive Image.
6. P-Type is the Project Editor. Simply named after program such as PSD.
Pro/Con

The only real Pro/Con here is JPG/PNG and even then it is really not even Pro/Con but merely purpose. With that said :

JPG is far Superior in Quality however Inferior in Size.
PNG is far Superior in Size however can be Equivalent in Quality.
WebP varies and more of a default creating much more work with very little benefit.

As Options here are just standard.

JPG/PNG Differences

JPG dominates in Showcase/Slideshow such as Photography/HD - with pixels comes great filesize. This is not really a problem until rendering, transferring, uploading, and downloading. Where the problem caused is by data of all those Pixels.

Sure one could compress the JPG to make it smaller - but compression has already reduced pixels therefore taken utility out of JPG. Enter PNG which is way more versatile. PNG is my default for this very reason, which also gives the option to remove backgrounds and Layer. Although the compromise is normally resolution.

All in all JPG should only be used for personal collections/Originals/Production/Showcase.
Png on the other hand is for quite everything else except Animation, SVG, or P-Type

Where does this leave WEBP?
WEBP is going to be most of the download images from the WEB unless pulled from the actual source.
You could use WEBP for even further compression - not advised for such little benefit as I recommend to just convert to PNG.

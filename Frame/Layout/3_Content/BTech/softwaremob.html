Open Source

Open Source is generally free and the code is public; meaning you will usually have full access to the program's code. This has many advantages and many flaws if you are not a developer or understand the language the program is written in to tweak, fix bugs, or create new features & updates.

Although, the pro is even if you cant develop it further than the original - then it is just another program that is free and usable at your discretion 99% of the time.

Proprietary

Proprietary Software (not Open Source), such as Windows/Apple and other big name brand Softwares/Services/Products are not free and brings in the laws of pirating, terms/conditions, and legalities of using a software.

Licensing goes further than just downloading, installing, and using a software/product but it can/does extend to "content" created with the software/product in some cases - it is crucial one reads the terms and services.

Just because you bought/purchased the software does not mean you own it(you normally don't) or even guarantees to grant you the creative rights or royalties to anything you create with it in some instances.

When explaining Perpetual/Leasing and Subscription

Perpetual

Let me start off by stating clearly I do not lease software nor do I purchase subscriptions aka Cloud garbage;

unless they fall into specific niches(services), such as: Email, VPN, Hosting, Domains and the like.

I will only spend money on general software that I find myself rationalizing the reasons as to why I need a specific tool or program, at a specific cost/ratio of longevity.

I hope this sort of sums up a Perpetual License by example.

Leasing

Leasing is normally just a Perpetual License. Yet, NOT ALWAYS the case. In many cases it means exactly that (Leasing) and no different than a Subscription(Renting). This marketing term(Hype) is just that, is normally offered when you purchase a Perpetual License in which grants access to future updates(read the terms of software). Normally these updates are time sensitive(introductory) such as 6 months/1 year hence a Lease; again read the terms of the software.

Meanwhile, when the Lease expires you still have the Perpetual License with no more updates making it sort of a rent to own concept.

However, if it was Subscription your software expires the moment your(Lease/Subscription) ends.

Subscription

This is 100% time sensitive. This is the absolute worst way possible for software and only creates a Perpetual Pay Cycle that will dictate when and how you use the software. If they say update your Software you update, if they say buy this then you buy it, if they say prices changed you pay it, if they say you have been revoked you been revoked - absolute opposite of a Perpetual License.

This is a catch 22. Many mistake when I speak in these terms I am against supporting a Dev Team. This is not true, I understand Development, Changes, Updates, Features, etc. I also understand incompetence, lazy, corporatism, and milking.

Subscription should only be for services no different than your utilities and Never Foundational Software.

See "Legacy Section" of why this is so important.

There is alot of overlap feature wise between the 4 programs, excluding Duplicate Cleaner - only Revo will allow us to go even deeper - far surpassing CCleaners uninstaller - yet nowhere close to CCleaners monitor, scan, and cleaning. As well as surpassing BleachBits registry deletion on uninstall - not BleachBits after uninstall. This should be your default Installer/Uninstaller.

Again, as CCleaner is named for monitoring and quick suite (Computer-Cleaner), BleachBit is named for Bleaching(or Wipeing), Revo Uninstallers name tells you exactly what it is and does (Installs and Uninstalls) - true uninstalls. Meaning, a ton of files get added to the registry with every install/uninstall you add and remove - which sit there in what is technically the graveyard of the computer for eternity due to nothing removes them (neither CCleaner/Bleachbit). This is where Revo Uninstaller shines tracing the Install and removing the traces - no-more having to play grave-keeper.

Although, as usual it comes with overlapping features such as "Evidence Remover"(Bleaching) and clean up(scan) it expands with access to Windows built-in tools which are powerful and often overlooked and scans built in for ease of access.

For a price of $30 and what it does; its a steal and that includes a portable to always have on hand.

Bulk Crap Uninstaller is a SOLID alternative. Open Source and Free. If you notice I didn't compare them, because Bulk Crap Uninstaller is just as good just not as beginner friendly as Revo.

CCleaner is a Freemium option (if you desire)- or $30. This can come to an end or "OS" incompatible if you run older OS.

The take away here is that CCleaner is not expensive for what it does and everything it does being a FlagShip. Essentially what I am saying is getting a back up copy now would be a wise choice. Although not at all really required - unless you want auto processes.

With that out of the way, CCleaner is a phenomenal piece of software that will not require all the updates that come with the Lease-Perpetual License because these particular softwares are tuned into the computers OS preferences - not cloud. That is critical to understand and keep in mind with all Software!!!

Meaning, even when your version expires (either Update License or by OS)... the License simply covers the "Download" and subscription to update for that Particular Version, not the program itself. This may change when in future updates with restructuring "OS"folders/names/locations in future updates. Essentially— it is a one time purchase that does not require the license to run and acts more as an Open Source donation for the time being — rather than a Corporate Software.

So why bother with BleachBit?

So now lets look to BleachBit being an Open Source that does not have the license/download dilemma; also lacks the possible OS limitations that would require version rollback for now - as unlike CCleaner will add Linux systems into the equation - as well as a portable, already you can see all the features BleachBit is bringing to the table.

Since it is Open Source, the OS limitation is not a problem (if it had ever been a problem). It is not limited by corporate pressure of Microsoft or any other's will. Anybody, can tweak update, modify, build, design etc their version of BleachBit. BleachBit also surpasses CCleaner in many regards; especially when we get into system jumps, portables, deletion, and features compared to CCleaner(Freemium). Although CCleaner(non-freemium) does outperform and surpass BleachBit across the functionality spectrum.

At Cost BleachBit is Champion, Portability Winner, Linux/Win Winner. Yet, if Win and you want the Auto Suite (automatically runs and cleans in background) CCleaner will become Champion.

This helps to really differentiate between the two programs and highlight their strengths and weaknesses - Also show how 2 programs doing essentially the same thing are Radically different and both just as Important.

An I/O

The IO

The IO is the back panel or any panel that connects. This is simply the Input(I) and Output(O). An example would be if you connect your Screen(Monitor) the image shows up because the signal is being Output to the Screen(Monitor), a Headphone Jack is another perfect example.

The Mouse and Keyboard are normally Inputs.

USB and these type of cords are normally both - an Input to put Data(Files) onto the Computer, but can act as an Output if transferring files from Out of the Computer and Into an external Drive or mobile device.

Ps/2 True

The processor(CPU) is not that good for something in 2 years you can pick up for practically nothing. See Specs above for recap - CPU depends on Socket - Socket determines the Board, Board calls on CPU that fits Socket. Really it all comes down to the IO and expanded by PCI with whatever you want except Ps/2. This is something that if it is not on the IO you should be very wary.

These Ps/2 connections are industry standard. I have failed to find a Board that couldn't run a Graphic Card, and 2 Pci for connections such as FireWire(or the like) which are driver driven - meaning the only thing to watch for is the Ps/2 port - these are those classic Green/Purple ports - today they are called Game ports and are Red or Black ; or Combo which are the multi-color port Half Green and Half Purple which you will need a Y-Cable splitter, then the Basic 2 port Green and Purple.

This Port is a darling you don't want to lose. Adding a Ps/2 card is not a true Ps/2 port, all USB Ps/2 are not TRUE Ps/2 ports for cards. add ons, etc and all USB makes the Ps/2 USB/Driver reliant. A True Ps/2 port is Ps/2 not USB!

To demonstrate this and show just how valuable this port is and that I am not just hyping you up and not agrreing with your favorite youtuber(corporate salesmen) and going against the grain - lets look at this Brand NEW motherBoard : The ASUS ROG Maximus Z690 Apex, which is an +$800 motherBoard with support for 12th Gen Intel Alder Lake chips, DDR5 RAM, and PCIe 5.0 connectivity. Now why would something so New, Latest, Greatest, Top Of The 2022 Line State of the ART - notably include a COMPLETE set of two color-coded PS/2 ports.

Who would want this "archaic", "old", and "outdated tech"? Why would they want it in this digital age of such Tech Supremacy? Let alone pay for it? Keep in mind everything said thus far across the entire spectrum.

 

Ps/2 does not require drivers of any kind and without it installing Windows7 on Boards without Ps/2 is impossible for drivers are required for USB. When you connect a USB keyBoard to the PC, for example, a driver must tell the computer what you are connecting to that port and they will not be found because they wont be installed.

However, when you connect a keyBoard/mouse to a Ps/2 port the PC knows that it is a keyBoard without needing to be told in any way. This means that a keyBoard/mouse connected to this port will always work, regardless of whether you are in the BIOS and/or what ever operating system you are using.

Ps/2 always works. In addition to the above, when you boot the PC the Ps/2 port loads even before the BIOS or processor data, and it literally has the power to interrupt CPU instructions. This means that it always works, no matter what you do, until you turn off the power. For this reason it is ideal for BIOS updates, firmware loads, and basically whatever you need - nothing else competes.

Ps/2 is inviolable . All of the above also means that it is inviolable. You cannot remotely access a Ps/2 peripheral in any way as there is no firmware, driver, or software involved. It is pure and hard hardware.

It has Full N-Key Rollover support . The famous feature of gaming keyBoards that allows to press as many keys simultaneously as we want without getting cluttered is inherent in Ps/2 ports. For this reason, many professional gamers prefer to use these types of ports instead of conventional USB.

Why are they taking it away - read this Ps/2 section again.

Ps/2 means you can opt out of upgrades, install Windows 7 or any thing you want to use, and never be corporate driver reliant. It is freedom to do whatever you want to do.

I have failed to find a Board that couldn't run a Graphic Card, and 2 Pci for connections such as FireWire(or the like) - meaning the only thing to watch for is the Ps/2 port these are the classic Green/Purple ports - today they are called combo/game ports and are Red/Black but if nothing else aim for multi-color port or better get Boards that have Both.

This Port is a darling you don't want to lose. Adding a Ps/2 card is not a true Ps/2 port, all USB Ps/2 are not TRUE Ps/2 ports these are USB/Driver reliant. A True Ps/2 port is not!

Why are they taking it away? Read the Ps/2 section again.

From here on out everything is purely function and aesthetics. The more you chase aesthetics such as the MotherBoard which is beautiful - the higher the price will go.

The cooling, fans, power supply, and case all have their functions and roles - but are quite generic and standardized from here on out.

I will say Noctua(Cooling/Fans) and Corsair(Power Supply) you can never go wrong with.
